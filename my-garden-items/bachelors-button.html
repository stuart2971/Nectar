Bachelor's Button

Appearance and Origin

Bachelor’s Button, or better known as corn flower, is a bright blue and purple flower.  It is mainly used as an accent plant in vegetable gardens.  Corn flowers got its nick name because they often appear in corn fields even when not intended to.  

This plant is native to European countries and mainly found by river banks, rail roads, ledges or bluffs.  

They have light blue to light purple lenceolate shaped pedals with grey-ish green stems.

What do They Like?

Like many other types of flowers, this is a very low maintenance plant.  It likes a lot of sun but very much water.  They do enjoy some shade, but 6 hours of directs sun light is fine too.  These flowers are perennial, which means they some back every year without having to replant them.  

Theirs seeds are hidden inside of the flower’s bulb.  Once the flower has willowed, the layer of dead pedals can be peeled back to reveal the bushel of flower seeds.  These seeds, in my opion, sort of look like mini blowdarts!

It’s Endangered?!

Since these flowers grew like weeds in corn fields, they soon became an endangered species of plants in Europe.  This is because of agriculture intensification, or the overuse of persicides.  Because of this, this plant is considered rare or even almost extinct in places, such as Ireland.

Gardening Tips

The best way to grow these plants is to keep them in one place.  From my experience, TRANSPLATING IS VERY RISKY.  I suggest to avoid growing them in a small pot only to transplant them into a bigger one or the ground.  Though, for the germination process, they can be placed on a damp paper towel in a warm and dark place, then once sprouted, placed into the ground. If that is too much work, placing the seeds straight into the ground is pretty much fool-proof.  

The best soil to use is one with multch this will help with maintaining the growth of weeds.  Also, watering the plants regularly is suggested to help inhibit insect infestations and fungal growth.
