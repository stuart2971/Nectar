About

Like any other root, garlic loves water and you can probably even get away with growing hydroponically.  If you do not have the materials to grow a plant hydroponically, I’ve got you covered.  Garlic is kind of a weird plant.  Instead of being planted in the spring, garlic prefers to be planted in late summer to early fall or when the season is just about to end.  

So really, it takes almost an entire year to mature.  Weird, right?  But it is totally worth it!  Garlic is very healthy for you and have many medicinal uses.  Garlic mainly works with the blood in your body.  It will help regulate your blood pressure and cholesterol and are known for its antibiotic properties.  Here’s a tip, if you eat lots of garlic when you are sick or have a cold, since it is very nutricious and has lots of health benefits, this will help get rid of your sickness quicker than it were to go away on its own.  

Not only is it great for its medicinal uses, but it is also used all around the world to add a delicious and unique flavour to food.  After harvesting, try cooking with some garlic! The flavour really comes out after cooking.  

How to Grow

There are, of course, many ways to plant garlic.  Personally, I like to grow garlic from the already sprouted ones that you can buy from the grocery store.  There is an option of using seeds, but I have not tried using them before.

So garlic likes the cold right? Here’s why.  

The best time to plant garlic is closer to the end of the season.  During and after winter, the ground freezes and defrsots the plants that are in the ground experience the “winter dorment period”.  Apparently, the winter dorment period is beneficial to the plant and this is what helps it germinate.  This is why garlic takes about 9 months to mature.  

On the bright side, now you will not have to try to guess when to plant the garlic and worry about missing the perfect time, because pretty much any time before the ground freezes is perfect!

Under the ground is where you can find the garlic bulbs, but above ground, they just look like a normal garden plant.  They can grow up to 4 feet tall and grow hermaphrodite flowers.  Like all other flowers, these plants are pollinated by insects.

Watering? Sun? Shade?

The best place to plant your garlic is somewhere sunny and partically shady.  Once the winter is over, the garlic should begin to grow on its own.  For now, watering may not be neccessary, however, once it matures more and the temperature begins to rise, you may need to start watering this plant very frequently.  

The plants like it sunny and can tolerate shade.  Remember, growing garlic is all about patience.  9 months is a pretty long time to wait for a single plant.
