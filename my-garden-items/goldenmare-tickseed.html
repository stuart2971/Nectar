Goldenmere Tickseed

Appearance

These flowers are very vibrant in colour, which attracts a lot of attention to it.  This flower is mainly used in gardens as a plant that attracts bugs and insects to keep them away from the other main crops or flowers in the garden.  

Tickseeds have both basal and cauline leaves, with bright yellow ray floret pedals with the attachment centre being red.  

This is considered a wildflower that is grown in many places, such as the prairies, plaines, meadows, pastures and savanna.  Basically, anywhere with a lot of sunlight.

How to Grow

The best times that these flowers bloom is in June and July.  This may be different depending on where you live and how hot the summers are, along with the amount of precipitation.  
‍
Want to grow this at home? Here are my tips (based on what I experienced from growing these flowers).

First, when I say they do not like much water, I mean they DO NOT like much water.  Overwatering the plant may result in slowing the growth of the flowers and delay its bloom. When first growing these flowers, I foolishly watered them almost every other day whether the soil was wet or not.  This made the blooming period get delayed by a month or 2, despite germinating them several months before.  

Secondly, the soil needs to be a certain mixture that you can just quickly mix up yourself.  If you do not have these materials, they can be bought at pretty much any plant/gardening store.   If this soil mixture is not used, this may slow down the maturing process of the flower.

Best Soil Mixture

The mixture has 2 main components to it:
1. Sand - sand is used to create more air pockets beneath the surface so the roots can get bigger, which will make the plant absorb more nutries and speed up the growing process.
2. Acidity - Soils natually contain acid, but most potting soil does not have enough for these flowers.  The range you are looking for is pH < 6.8, which is faily acidic.  Some things you can add to your soil to make it more acidic are sphagnum peat, organic mulches and acidifying nitrogen.  

Remember, this soil likes it dry.  DO NOT OVER WATER!! Let the rain water your plants.  If you live in a very dry area, check your soil every few days to see the moisture.  If the soil is very dry and crumbly, water until is it moist.

Goldenmeres really like the sun, try to avoid shady places, but a bit of shade will not do much harm!
