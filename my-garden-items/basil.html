basil

About

Basil is a great plant to grow either indoors or outside.  The natural greagrant the leaves release keeps bugs and insects far, far away.  Worried about bugs getting into your house? Basil is a natural insect repellant.  Just place the plant near your front door or opened windows and let the basil do the rest!  Because of this, that means your plant is less likely to get insect infestations or be eaten by bugs.  Of course, there are some insects that still eat basil, but at least there are less.  

Other than giving your pasta a gourmet flavour, basil can do so much more than that.  Like I said, they are a natural bug repellant, but they are also shown to ease anxiety, headaches, stomach pains and constipation from the scent alone.  

Another way to use basil to your benefit is by using it as an anti-acid, meant to help with digestion.  Just put some in your food or in your drinks and you’re good to go!

Appearance

Basil have medium sized, light green, silky, creased leaves.  They tend to grow big and bushy and a single plant can last an entire season.  When the basil is a rich green colour and large in size, they are most likely ready to be harvested.

Every so often, you must pluck off the white flowers that appear on the plants.  This is so the plant can grow bushier rather than taller. Not only do the flowers restrict the width of the plant, but they also disturb the taste.  After leaving the flowers on the plant for a while, the leaves will slowly start to loose its flavour. Try to pluck the flowers from the base of the stem to avoid having to pluck the same one everyday.  

Weather

Growing your own basil is not that difficult.  Either inside or outside is fine.  I prefer to grow inside when it is cooler out and outside when it’s hot.  

BASIL. CANNOT. HANDLE. COLD. WEATHER.  Try to keep them away from the cold, as they become very weak.  Basil plants love the sun. Try not to put them in the shade at all.  Preferably, at least 8 hours of direct sunlight everyday.

Soil

I highly recommend not using any type of fertilizer, as it may make the flavour less intense.  Yes, it will make your plant bushier, however, it will evenly make each leave weaker in flavour.  

As for soil, any good potting soil is usable.  Nothing fancy is required! But just remember NOT TO USE FERTILIZER.  Using quality of the soil is important because it will give you the best flavour.
