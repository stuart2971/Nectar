Crocus Sativus

 
 
 
 
 
 
About
 

Crocus Sativus, otherwise known as saffron, is the most expensive spice in the world.  For one pound of this spice, it can cost nearly up to $1500. On top of that, you would not believe how many flowers are needed to even make up a pound.  75 000 flowers!!!  

Saffron spice is made of the stigmas on the plant (the red things sticking out of the centre of the flower).  Each crocus flower only has 3 stigmas, meaning which means that 75 000 flowers are required to make a single pound of saffron.  

The reason why I want to try growing it is because I have never tried real or authenic saffron before.  Since the demand is so high, I decided that I want to give it a try.

 
Watering? Sun? Shade?
 

Saffron flowers, believe it or not, are very low maintenance and pretty much grow on its own.  They like a lot of sun, no shade and very little water.  Open fields are the most ideal place to grow a saffron plant.

 
How to Grow
 

These plants grow from bulbs instead of seeds, which means there is really only one way to germinate them.  Simply place them into the ground and give it a bit of water.  

After approximately 8 weeks, the flowers should  be ready to be harvested.  If not, be sure to keep a close eye on them.  Remember, these flowers should have exactly 3 stigmas when they are matured.  If they do not, they are either not real crocus bulbs or you just lost $4.

 
Harvesting
 

To harvest, flock out the entire flower (make sure you grab the stigmas) and collect them into  basket.  On the same day (to preserve freshness), separate the pedals from the red stigmas and place them on a large flat try.  The flowers do have similar looking yellow stigmas.  THESE ARE NOT SAFFRON.  Some companies include it, but that will just make your saffon weaker in taste.  

After placing your stigmas onto a tray, let them dry in a cool dark place.  Once they are completely dried up, you now have ready to use saffron! This spice can be stored for an even longer time if you keep it in an air tight container. 
Every year, after harvest, there can be up to double the amount of crocus bulbs found.  This means that they multiply each year, giving you even more bulbs than you started with!
